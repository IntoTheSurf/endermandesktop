Basics

Breezily and Witchly are very similar methods. Both are popular and medium difficulty to learn.
These methods use strafing (moving left and right) instead of crouching to avoid falling off.
Witchly is slightly faster than Breezily.

Direct bridge instructions

1. Place down a block and get on top of it.
2. Crouch and go backwards until you hit the edge of the block.
3. Still holding crouch, look down and position yourself diagonally.
4. Once you've placed a new block, let go of crouch and hold the S and D or A and S buttons, while also occassionally pressing A or D, depending which button youre not using.
5. You can either time your clicks or keep clicking. Use butterfly or dragclick.

Tip! Find the correct angle for bridging. Falling off only becomes more likely as methods become more difficult

Diagonal bridge instructions

1. Place down a block and get on top of it.
2. Crouch and go backwards until you hit the edge of the block.
3. Still holding crouch, look down and position yourself at a harsher angle than for direct.
4. Once you've placed two new blocks and let go of crouch and hold the S and D or A and S buttons, while also occassionally pressing A or D, depending which button youre not using.
5. You can either time your clicks or keep clicking. Use butterfly or dragclick.

More tips:

1. You will fall off a lot at the beginning. The more you practice, the less it will happen. Even pros still fall
2. Jumping at the right moments is important. YOu can jump ever 3 to 7 blocks
